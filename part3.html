IELTS Speaking — Part 3 (50 Questions)

Har bir savolga Band-9 namunaviy javob va foydali gaplar (useful phrases). Savolni bosing.

1. Why do people often move from rural to urban areas?
Discuss causes and effects.

Sample: People migrate to cities primarily for better economic opportunities, education and services. Urban areas concentrate jobs and infrastructure, which attracts those seeking improved living standards, though this can create pressure on housing and public services.

Useful: "economic opportunities", "concentrate jobs and services", "pressure on housing"
2. How can societies encourage young people to participate in politics?

Sample: Encouraging youth participation requires civic education, accessible platforms for engagement and policies that address issues young people care about. When young citizens see tangible impacts from their involvement, turnout and activism increase.

Useful: "civic education", "tangible impacts", "youth engagement"
3. What role does media play in shaping public opinion?

Sample: Media frames issues by selecting which stories to highlight and how to present them, thereby influencing perceptions and priorities. Responsible journalism can inform the public, whereas biased reporting may polarise debates.

Useful: "frame issues", "highlight stories", "polarise debates"
4. Is it important to protect endangered languages?

Sample: Protecting endangered languages preserves cultural identity and unique worldviews encoded in language. Support through education and media helps revitalise usage among younger generations.

Useful: "preserve cultural identity", "unique worldviews", "revitalise usage"
5. How does globalization affect local traditions?

Sample: Globalization can both erode and enrich local traditions: exposure brings new influences that may dilute customs, yet cultural exchange can revitalise interest and create hybrid forms.

Useful: "erode and enrich", "cultural exchange", "hybrid forms"
6. What are the advantages of bilingual education?

Sample: Bilingual education enhances cognitive flexibility, improves communication skills and opens employment opportunities. It also fosters cross-cultural understanding in increasingly diverse societies.

Useful: "cognitive flexibility", "opens employment opportunities", "cross-cultural understanding"
7. How should schools prepare students for the modern workplace?

Sample: Schools should combine foundational knowledge with transferable skills like critical thinking, teamwork and digital literacy. Practical experience through projects and internships bridges theory and workplace demands.

Useful: "transferable skills", "digital literacy", "bridge theory and practice"
8. Do you think technology makes life better or more complicated?

Sample: Technology improves convenience and access to information, yet it can complicate life through distraction, privacy concerns and over-dependence. The net effect depends on how individuals and societies manage its use.

Useful: "access to information", "privacy concerns", "manage its use"
9. What is the impact of advertising on consumer behaviour?

Sample: Advertising shapes preferences by associating products with lifestyle and emotion, often stimulating demand beyond need. Media literacy helps consumers make more informed choices.

Useful: "shape preferences", "stimulate demand", "media literacy"
10. How can cities become more sustainable?

Sample: Cities can become sustainable by investing in public transport, green spaces and energy-efficient infrastructure, while encouraging waste reduction and circular economy practices.

Useful: "public transport", "energy-efficient infrastructure", "circular economy"
11. What are the effects of long working hours on society?

Sample: Long working hours can reduce productivity, harm mental and physical health, and strain family relationships. Policies promoting work-life balance improve wellbeing and sustainable productivity.

Useful: "work-life balance", "reduce productivity", "harm mental health"
12. How important is lifelong learning?

Sample: Lifelong learning is essential in a rapidly changing job market; continuous skill updates maintain employability and personal growth. It encourages adaptability and curiosity throughout life.

Useful: "rapidly changing job market", "maintain employability", "personal growth"
13. Should governments regulate social media?

Sample: Some regulation is necessary to protect privacy and prevent misinformation, but it must balance free speech and avoid excessive censorship. Transparent rules and independent oversight are key.

Useful: "prevent misinformation", "balance free speech", "independent oversight"
14. How can tourism be made sustainable?

Sample: Sustainable tourism involves limiting environmental impact, supporting local economies and respecting cultural heritage. Policies like visitor caps and community involvement help preserve destinations.

Useful: "environmental impact", "support local economies", "community involvement"
15. What are the challenges of aging populations?

Sample: Aging populations strain healthcare systems and pension schemes while reducing the workforce ratio. Solutions include encouraging longer working lives, improving eldercare and adjusting fiscal policies.

Useful: "strain healthcare systems", "longer working lives", "adjust fiscal policies"
16. Is art important in education?

Sample: Art nurtures creativity, emotional intelligence and cultural awareness, complementing STEM subjects. Including arts in curricula develops well-rounded thinkers and problem solvers.

Useful: "nurture creativity", "emotional intelligence", "well-rounded thinkers"
17. How should companies respond to climate change?

Sample: Companies should invest in low-carbon operations, adopt sustainable supply chains and disclose environmental impacts. Corporate responsibility combined with innovation can drive decarbonisation.

Useful: "low-carbon operations", "sustainable supply chains", "disclose impacts"
18. What makes a good leader?

Sample: A good leader has vision, empathy and integrity; they communicate clearly, empower others and lead by example. Such qualities build trust and motivate teams to achieve shared goals.

Useful: "lead by example", "empower others", "build trust"
19. How important is privacy in the digital age?

Sample: Privacy is crucial as personal data can be misused; safeguarding it preserves autonomy and prevents harm. Strong regulation and user awareness are both necessary.

Useful: "personal data", "preserve autonomy", "user awareness"
20. Can sport unite people from different backgrounds?

Sample: Sport can bridge cultural divides by fostering teamwork, shared rituals and community pride. Major events create common experiences that transcend individual differences.

Useful: "bridge cultural divides", "teamwork", "shared rituals"
21. How does diet affect public health?

Sample: Diet plays a central role in chronic disease prevalence; poor nutrition increases risks of obesity, diabetes and heart disease. Public education and access to healthy food are essential preventive measures.

Useful: "chronic disease", "public education", "preventive measures"
22. What is the value of volunteering?

Sample: Volunteering builds social capital, provides practical help to communities and enhances participants' skills and empathy. It fosters civic responsibility and mutual support.

Useful: "social capital", "practical help", "civic responsibility"
23. Do you think remote work will continue to grow?

Sample: Remote work is likely to persist because of technological readiness and employee preference for flexibility, though hybrid models may dominate to balance collaboration and autonomy.

Useful: "technological readiness", "employee preference", "hybrid models"
24. How can we reduce plastic waste?

Sample: Reducing plastic waste needs policy measures like bans on single-use plastics, improved recycling and consumer behaviour change toward reusable alternatives.

Useful: "single-use plastics", "recycling infrastructure", "consumer behaviour"
25. What role does science play in policymaking?

Sample: Science provides evidence and predictive tools that inform policy choices, especially in health and environment, but policymakers must consider social values and feasibility too.

Useful: "provide evidence", "predictive tools", "social values"
26. Is it important to preserve historical buildings?

Sample: Preserving historical buildings safeguards cultural heritage and provides continuity with the past, while careful restoration can support tourism and education.

Useful: "safeguard cultural heritage", "careful restoration", "support tourism"
27. How can technology improve education?

Sample: Technology personalises learning through adaptive platforms, increases access via online courses and facilitates active engagement with multimedia resources.

Useful: "personalise learning", "adaptive platforms", "multimedia resources"
28. What are the causes of crime in cities?

Sample: Crime often stems from inequality, lack of opportunity and social exclusion; targeted social programmes, policing reforms and community initiatives can mitigate risks.

Useful: "social exclusion", "targeted programmes", "policing reforms"
29. Should cultural events be funded by the state?

Sample: Public funding for culture ensures broad access, nurtures creative talent and strengthens national identity, though accountability and diverse support models are important.

Useful: "public funding", "nurture talent", "broad access"
30. How can we encourage healthy eating?

Sample: Encouraging healthy diets requires education, affordable fresh produce, clear labelling and incentives for retailers to stock nutritious options.

Useful: "affordable fresh produce", "clear labelling", "nutritious options"
31. What impact do celebrities have on young people?

Sample: Celebrities can inspire positive social action but may also set unrealistic standards; media literacy and parental guidance help young people interpret celebrity influence critically.

Useful: "unrealistic standards", "media literacy", "parental guidance"
32. Is it better to travel alone or in a group?

Sample: Both have merits: solo travel encourages independence and introspection, while group travel provides shared experiences and safety; choice depends on goals.

Useful: "shared experiences", "independence", "depends on goals"
33. How important is scientific research for economic growth?

Sample: Scientific research drives innovation, productivity and new industries; investment in R&D correlates strongly with long-term economic competitiveness.

Useful: "drives innovation", "investment in R&D", "economic competitiveness"
34. What are the benefits of reading books?

Sample: Reading improves empathy, vocabulary and critical thinking, and provides deep engagement that other media often lack, contributing to lifelong learning.

Useful: "lifelong learning", "critical thinking", "deep engagement"
35. How can communities support mental health?

Sample: Communities can support mental health by fostering social connections, providing accessible services and reducing stigma through awareness campaigns.

Useful: "social connections", "reduce stigma", "accessible services"
36. Is it necessary to teach ethics at school?

Sample: Teaching ethics helps students develop moral reasoning and navigate complex dilemmas they will face in society and work; practical case studies make lessons relevant.

Useful: "moral reasoning", "complex dilemmas", "practical case studies"
37. How do economic crises affect people's behaviour?

Sample: Economic crises often lead to reduced consumption, increased saving and stress, while prompting policy shifts; social safety nets can buffer adverse effects.

Useful: "reduced consumption", "increased saving", "social safety nets"
38. What is the role of museums in society?

Sample: Museums preserve heritage, educate the public and spark curiosity; modern museums also engage communities through interactive displays and outreach.

Useful: "preserve heritage", "spark curiosity", "interactive displays"
39. How can governments reduce unemployment?

Sample: Governments can reduce unemployment by stimulating demand, investing in training and education, and supporting entrepreneurship and small businesses.

Useful: "stimulating demand", "invest in training", "support entrepreneurship"
40. Are traditional crafts important in modern economies?

Sample: Traditional crafts sustain cultural identity and can be economically valuable through niche markets and tourism, especially when artisans gain market access and training.

Useful: "sustain cultural identity", "niche markets", "market access"
41. How does migration affect sending and receiving countries?

Sample: Migration can boost skills and remittances in sending countries and address labour shortages in receiving countries, but it may also raise integration and social cohesion challenges.

Useful: "remittances", "labour shortages", "social cohesion"
42. What is the impact of peer pressure on teenagers?

Sample: Peer pressure influences identity and choices strongly; positive peer groups can motivate achievement, whereas negative pressure may lead to risky behaviour.

Useful: "peer pressure", "positive peer groups", "risky behaviour"
43. How can technology help people with disabilities?

Sample: Assistive technologies such as screen readers, voice control and prosthetics increase independence and inclusion, enabling greater participation in education and work.

Useful: "assistive technologies", "increase independence", "greater participation"
44. What makes a city attractive to investors?

Sample: Investors seek stable governance, good infrastructure, skilled labour and favourable regulatory environments; such factors reduce risk and improve returns.

Useful: "stable governance", "skilled labour", "favourable regulatory environment"
45. How important is scientific literacy for the public?

Sample: Scientific literacy enables citizens to evaluate claims, make informed health and environmental choices, and support evidence-based policies.

Useful: "evaluate claims", "evidence-based policies", "informed choices"
46. Should schools focus more on vocational training?

Sample: Emphasising vocational training alongside academic education equips students with practical skills and meets labour market needs, improving employment prospects for many young people.

Useful: "vocational training", "practical skills", "labour market needs"
47. How do festivals contribute to society?

Sample: Festivals strengthen community bonds, celebrate cultural identity and generate economic activity through tourism and local spending.

Useful: "community bonds", "cultural identity", "economic activity"
48. What are the pros and cons of online education?

Sample: Online education increases accessibility and flexibility, but it can reduce social interaction and may suffer from engagement and assessment integrity issues.

Useful: "increases accessibility", "reduce social interaction", "assessment integrity"
49. How can young entrepreneurs be supported?

Sample: Young entrepreneurs benefit from access to finance, mentorship, incubators and a regulatory environment that lowers barriers to startup formation.

Useful: "access to finance", "mentorship", "lower barriers"
50. What personal qualities are important for success?

Sample: Resilience, adaptability and effective communication are key qualities for success; combined with a willingness to learn, they enable people to navigate change and seize opportunities.

Useful: "resilience", "adaptability", "willingness to learn"
